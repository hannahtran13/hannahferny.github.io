Do Transformers Learn Rules
or Just Patterns?

Investigating whether modern language models actually learn underlying structure—or rely on statistical shortcuts.

Context

Transformer models perform extremely well on sequence tasks—but it remains unclear whether they truly learn the rules of a language or simply approximate patterns within the training distribution.


Approach

I trained GPT-style models on formal languages generated by deterministic finite automata (DFAs), using a next-token prediction objective. I evaluated performance across in-distribution and out-of-distribution sequences to test whether models generalize rule-based structure.


Key Insight

While models perform well within the training distribution, performance degrades significantly on longer or unseen sequences—suggesting reliance on pattern recognition rather than true rule learning.


Why It Matters

This work highlights limitations in how current AI systems generalize, raising important questions about reliability, interpretability, and the boundaries of machine learning systems.
